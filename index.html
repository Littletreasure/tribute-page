Timeline of General Booth's life

  • 1829 - Born in Sneinton, Nottingham.
  • 1842 - Apprenticed to a pawnbroker.
  • 1844 - Converted to Christianity.
  • 1849 - Moved to London and found work with a pawnbroker. Took to open air evangelising in the streets and on Kennington Common.
  • 1851 - Joined the Methodist Reform Church.
  • 1852 - Left pawnbroking and became a full time preacher in Clapham. Became engaged to Catherine Mumford.
  • 1855 - Married Catherine Mumford in London.
  • 1861 - Resigned from the Methodist Church and became an indpendent evangelist.
  • 1865 - Alongside his wife, opened The Christian Revival Society (later renamed the Christian Mission).
  • 1878 - The Christian Mission became known as The Salvation Army.
  • 1912 - Died, aged 83 at Hadley Wood, London.

During his lifetime, William Booth established Army work in 58 countries and colonies, travelling extensively and holding, "salvation meetings." He authored several books and wrote a number of songs. The Salvation Army and William Booth faced a great deal of opposition during the early years but opinion later changed to favour.

"[Booth's] long life and great talents were dedicated to the noble work of helping the poor and weak and giving them another chance to attain success and happiness."

- United States President William Taft
